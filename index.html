I'm Kaifa,

I am a PhD student in College of Design, Construction and Planning at University of Florida, U.S. I hold a Master degree in Transportation Engineering from Shanghai Jiao Tong University and a B.S. of Transportation Engineering from Central South University.
My research interest includes transportation network modeling and optimization, environmental analysis of transportation infrastructure (e.g. urban elevated expressways), analysis and evaluation of transportation policy, adaptation planning for climate change, urban resilience, autonomous vehicle AV-based microtransit, micromobility, machine learning and deep learning.

Urban Data Analytics

  • House Prices in Boulder

    Wrangling data with web-APIs, this project used R to develop a regression model with neighborhood effects to predict house prices in Boulder, CO.

  • COVID-19's Impact on Human Mobility in New York

    Based on Python, this study focuses on the spatio-temporal changes of mobility pattern in March 2019, March 2020 and March 2021 in New York City and analyzes the social equity issues caused by the pandemic.

  • Evictions and NDVI in Philadelphia

    Focusing on urban and suburban Philadelphia, this study analyzed the spatial distribution of evictions and NDVI values based on Landsat image.

Machine Learning

  • Multi-class Land Use Type Segmentation based on UNet

    Based on Pytorch, this project developed UNet architecture to classify the land use type of satellite images based on pixel level.

  • Landslide Susceptibility based on Machine Learning models with GAN

    Based on generative adversarial networks(GAN), this project synthesizes inventory data to solve the data scarsity issues in data-driven machine learning models, and increases their prediction capacity for landslide susceptibility in Hong Kong.

  • People Based Machine Learning-Housing Subsidy Prediction

    Based on R, this model developed a logic model to predict if homeowners will take the home repair tax credict or not. It also develops cost/benefit analysis to provide accurate and efficient resource allocation method to help governors save money.

Transportation Engineering

  • Bicycle and Pedestrian Plan for Eakins Oval and Fairmount, Philadelphia

    This projec is a transportation planning study that focuses on improving the safety, accessibility, and connectivity of the area for pedestrians and bicyclists.

  • Case Study: Kansas City Streetcar Main Street Extention project

    This project provides a comphrensive analysis of the KC street main street extention project. It reviewed the development history, and evaluated the effective and efficiency of the transportation systems based on population, land use, and economic affects

  • Crime Prediction in Chicago

    This project develops a drug crime prediction model in Chicago based on aggravated data with 500ft*500ft grid cell. It not only focuses on the prediction accuracy, but also discusses the selection bias and fairness in geospatial risk-predicted models.
